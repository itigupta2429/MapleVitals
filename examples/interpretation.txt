## **The Clearest Pattern: The Divergence**

**Young Albertan females aged 18–34 have experienced a dramatic, volatile increase in reporting fair or poor mental health since 2020, while their male peers have stabilized—creating a striking widening gender gap.**

### **The Numbers:**

| Metric | Males | Females |
|--------|-------|---------|
| **2015** | 5.1% | 8.1% |
| **2024** | 16.8% | 26.3% |
| **Total change** | +11.7 pp (+229%) | +18.2 pp (+225%) |
| **Gender gap 2015** | 3.0 pp | — |
| **Gender gap 2024** | 9.5 pp | — |

### **The Three Phases:**

1. **2015–2018: Steady rise for both** (Males 5.1%→9.7%, Females 8.1%→10.5%)  
   Both genders increasing gradually and in parallel.

2. **2019–2020: Inflection point** (COVID-19 era)  
   - Females spiked to 16.1% in 2019, then dipped to 13.0% in 2020  
   - Males steadily climbed to 11.8%

3. **2021–2024: Dramatic divergence**  
   - **Males stabilized** at 16.9%–16.8% (locked in from 2021 onward)  
   - **Females surged** from 21.0% (2021) → 34.5% (2023), then partial recovery to 26.3% (2024)

### **Why This Matters:**

The female peak of **34.5% in 2023** is more than **double the 2015 baseline** (8.1%), while males less than tripled theirs. Females also show **3× greater volatility** (std. dev. 8.4 vs. 4.4 pp for males), suggesting their mental health trajectory post-2020 has been more unstable. The gender gap itself tripled from 3.0 pp to 9.5 pp, indicating **sex-specific mental health pressures in this age group**—particularly acute for young women.
